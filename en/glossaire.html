Glossary

Essential Definitions to Understand the Beauty Industry

Virtual Ambassadors

Fully digital avatars created to promote branded products on social media.

Avatar

A digital representation of a user, often customizable with various virtual makeup styles.

Motion Capture

Technology that records an actor's movements to apply them to a digital character.

Makeup Democratization

Increasing accessibility of makeup products and techniques to a broader audience through digital technologies.

AR Filters (Augmented Reality)

Digital effects overlaid on reality, allowing visual elements like makeup to be added in real-time.

Artificial Intelligence (AI)

A system capable of simulating human intelligence, used to personalize looks based on various factors.

Virtual Makeup

The use of digital technologies to simulate makeup looks on images or videos, often via apps.

NFT (Non-Fungible Token)

A unique digital asset recorded on a blockchain, which may include filters or virtual makeup looks.

Aesthetic Standards

Sociocultural standards defining what is considered beautiful or attractive in a given society.

Makeup Prosthetics

Elements used to modify an actor's physical appearance, often used in cinema before the digital era.

Advertising Regulations

The establishment of laws to regulate the use of beauty filters in advertising to ensure transparency and authenticity.

Technology Watch

Monitoring emerging advancements in the field of virtual beauty to stay up to date with market trends.
